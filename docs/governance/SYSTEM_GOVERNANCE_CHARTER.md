# System Governance Charter

## Purpose
Execalc is a governed executive intelligence platform designed to convert unstructured information into structured, auditable strategic decisions.

The system exists to:
- improve executive decision quality
- preserve organizational reasoning
- synthesize strategic signals across organizations
- provide traceable decision intelligence
- prevent ungoverned AI usage inside enterprise environments

Execalc is designed to function as industrial-grade organizational cognition.

---

## Governance Principles

### Governance Above Generation
Execalc enforces structured reasoning above the language model layer. The language model produces language; Execalc governs judgment.

### Deterministic Reasoning
Critical reasoning steps are executed by deterministic software modules rather than prompts. The system must follow the ordered pipeline defined by the Judgment Kernel architecture.

### Auditability by Design
Strategic decisions may be recorded through the decision journal. Decision artifacts must be structured so organizations can review how conclusions were reached.

### Tenant Sovereignty
Each organization controls its namespace, data, and decision history. Execalc does not expose tenant information outside the tenant boundary.

### Security First
Cross-tenant communication is prohibited unless explicitly mediated through the defined interaction model. External signals must pass through the Porch layer before entering a tenant runtime.

---

## System Boundaries
Execalc is an advisory decision intelligence platform.

- The system does not autonomously execute financial transactions.
- The system does not directly modify enterprise infrastructure.
- The system does not bypass governance constraints through prompt instructions or model outputs.
- External signals may not enter a tenant runtime without Porch inspection.

---

## Data Governance

### Tenant Data Ownership
All data supplied to Execalc remains the property of the tenant organization.

### Data Isolation
Tenant data is logically isolated from other tenants. Isolation is enforced through namespace boundaries and system-level access controls.

### Decision Artifacts
Organizations may choose to persist decision records in a structured decision journal. Decision artifacts may include:
- scenario classification
- applied strategic overlays
- activated reflexes
- key assumptions
- confidence level
- recommended actions

### Data Export
Tenant organizations retain the ability to export their decision artifacts and system outputs.

---

## Decision Traceability
Execalc is designed to support auditable reasoning. When enabled, decision journal entries provide structured explanations of the reasoning path used to generate recommendations.

Organizations can review:
- why a recommendation was produced
- what assumptions were used
- which frameworks influenced the outcome

Traceability transforms AI output into decision intelligence infrastructure.

---

## System Evolution Rules
The integrity of Execalc depends on maintaining its architectural principles. The following components are governed system foundations:
- the Prime Directive reasoning framework
- the Judgment Kernel order-of-operations
- tenant isolation mechanisms
- Porch and Bridge interaction protocols

Changes to these components require formal architectural review. Governance rules may not be bypassed through prompt instructions or model outputs.

---

## Governance Objective
The objective of the Execalc governance model is to ensure organizations can safely deploy advanced decision intelligence while maintaining security, transparency, and institutional control.

Execalc is designed to function as a governed cognitive infrastructure layer for modern organizations.
