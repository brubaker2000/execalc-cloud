# Build Doctrine Addendum

## Purpose

This document captures doctrine lines that surfaced in session history and have not yet been distributed into their primary canonical homes. They are recorded here to prevent loss pending formal promotion.

All lines below are admitted as doctrine candidates. They should be promoted into their target docs during the next canonization pass.

---

## Category and Identity

**The product posture keeps rising:**
> From chatbot → governed synthesis engine → strategic barometer → intelligence platform.

This is not feature creep. It is category clarification. Each step up removes a ceiling from the addressable market.

**The cloud effort is a cloning mission, not a reinvention mission.**

The sandbox proved the brain works. The cloud is the delivery vehicle. Do not reinvent the architecture in the cloud. Reproduce it faithfully.

> "The sandbox proved the brain works; the cloud is the delivery vehicle."

---

## Build Law

**Build law is consistency before cleverness.**

A consistent governed implementation of the locked architecture is worth more than a clever deviation from it.

**The system should be built from explicit doctrine and scaffolding, not reconstructed from vibes.**

Vibes-based reconstruction is how drift starts. Doctrine and scaffolding are the antidote.

**The architecture was frozen before the codebase was complete. The doctrine is ahead of the implementation.**

This is not a problem. It is the correct sequence. The codebase catches up to the doctrine — not the other way around.

**The correct build sequence:**
1. Verify repo reality
2. Wire middleware / router
3. Tenant isolation
4. Memory service
5. Heuristic scanning
6. Thinker / MDL routing
7. Prime Directive gating
8. Recursive audit hooks
9. User-facing surfaces last

User-facing surfaces are the last thing built, not the first.

---

## Architectural Drift — The Primary Risk

> "The biggest recurring risk is not lack of ideas. It is architectural drift caused by summarization, renaming, and soft reinterpretation."

Drift enters the system through seemingly harmless acts: shortening a name, softening a distinction, combining two concepts for convenience. Each act is small. The cumulative effect is a system that no longer matches its doctrine.

Living registries, strict naming, and hard taxonomy boundaries exist specifically to resist this.

**Drift from canon was already showing up in conversation.** That is why this addendum exists.

---

## Developer Doctrine

**Dev partners are not supposed to co-architect the system. They are supposed to faithfully implement the locked architecture.**

The architecture is locked. The developer's job is implementation fidelity, not architectural judgment.

**The wrong developer is one who simplifies away the tank-grade governance spine.**

Simplification that removes governance is not engineering pragmatism. It is product destruction.

**A final audit/lock step is required before declaring cloud v1 ready.**

The Logic Stack Audit Template is a real gating concept, not decorative process. No v1 declaration without it.

---

## Cartridges — Scope Boundary

**Cartridges are situational overlays only. They are not the permanent thought-leader layer.**

The Monolith is the permanent thought-leader layer. Cartridges are domain-specific, situational, and tenant-scoped. The distinction must hold in schema, in activation logic, and in how cartridges are described to operators.

---

## Runtime Admission

**Runtime participants need admission criteria. Nothing gets into runtime just because it sounds useful.**

Every framework, heuristic, thinker, and cartridge must pass an admission evaluation before it activates in runtime. Sounding useful is not an admission criterion.

---

## Security Posture

**Security is a product-defining feature, not a back-office concern.**

**The security doctrine is sealed-vault isolation:**
- no shared memory shortcuts
- no reflex bleed
- no cross-tenant contamination
- no casual dev exposure to customer reasoning data

The security posture is part of the value proposition, not a compliance checkbox.

---

## Revenue and Demo Strategy

**Revenue does not require waiting for full SaaS completion.**

Controlled pilots, demos, and brief kits can finance the build. A one-user-at-a-time or solo-tenant demo mode is strategically acceptable as an early proving ground.

**Live demos are a strategic sales weapon**, especially in high-pressure, time-compressed scenarios. The Nashville draft room is the named archetype: a scenario where governed synthesis under time pressure demonstrates value that no static deck can replicate.

---

## The Exterior Stack

The exterior stack is a formal concept: integrations, dashboards, routing, and pluggable drivers live **outside** the judgment core.

The judgment core does not reach out. The exterior stack delivers context in and results out. This separation is not optional.

**Kubernetes and heavy DevOps theater are not the product.** Logistics must stay subordinate to governed runtime fidelity.
