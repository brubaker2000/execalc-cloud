# GAQP Core Principles
**Status:** Canonized | **Version:** 1.0 | **Authority:** Execalc GAQP Standards Body

## The Ten Principles

### 1. Materiality of Judgment
Not all qualitative inputs carry equal weight. Distinguish decision-relevant factors from noise.

### 2. Source Traceability
Every qualitative conclusion must be traceable to an identifiable input. No floating assertions.

### 3. Consistency of Framework Application
If a lens is applied in one period, it must be applied in subsequent periods.

### 4. The Conservatism Principle
When signals conflict, weight the more cautious interpretation. Optimistic readings must be explicitly justified.

### 5. Contextual Boundary Disclosure
Every conclusion must disclose conditions under which it is valid and under which it breaks down.

### 6. Multi-Source Corroboration
One read is an opinion. Three independent frameworks converging is a finding.

### 7. Judgment Independence
The analyst must be structurally separated from incentives that distort the conclusion.
Enforced in Execalc through the Prime Directive.

### 8. Proportionality
Depth of analysis must be proportional to the stakes of the decision.

### 9. The Revision Standard
Conclusions must define in advance what new evidence would require revision.
If nothing could change it, it is bias.

### 10. Scenario Completeness
Every assessment must account for principal alternative interpretations.
Steelman the opposing read.

## Relationship to the Prime Directive
The Ten Principles govern how claims are formed and stored.
The Prime Directive governs whether a proposed output should be delivered.
Sequential, not redundant.
