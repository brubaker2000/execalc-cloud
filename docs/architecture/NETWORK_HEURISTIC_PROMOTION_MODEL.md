# Network Heuristic Promotion Model

## Purpose

This document defines the model by which locally discovered heuristics can be promoted through a governed path into network-level enterprise intelligence.

---

## The Core Insight

Heuristics discovered in one organizational context often have relevance beyond that context.

A heuristic that helps a regional sales team close deals may contain transferable logic applicable across industries, geographies, or function types.

But without a governed promotion pathway, that heuristic stays local — locked inside the team, the individual, or the engagement that produced it.

**Execalc creates the governed path by which local heuristics become institutional and eventually network-level assets.**

---

## The Ford / Dealership Analogy

Consider a dealership network.

An individual dealership may discover a highly effective customer handling approach. Without a promotion mechanism, that discovery benefits only that location and eventually dissipates when the people involved move on.

With a network promotion model:
1. The dealership's heuristic is observed and evaluated
2. If it clears the materiality and durability threshold, it becomes a candidate
3. It is reviewed for generalizability — does this apply beyond this location?
4. If promoted, it becomes part of the Ford network intelligence — available to all dealerships
5. Over time, the network accumulates an inventory of tested, operational intelligence from real experience

The key: the heuristic originated locally and was promoted deliberately, not extracted by fiat or overridden by corporate assumption.

## The Three Heuristic Domains

### 1. Operator Heuristics
Rules of thumb, judgment patterns, and decision shortcuts developed and used by individual operators.

Characteristics:
- highly personal
- often unarticulated
- frequently valuable
- often domain-specific

Promotion path: operator-directed → GAQP admission evaluation → governed candidate → promoted to tenant-level if generalizable

### 2. Organizational Heuristics
Judgment patterns that have proven effective inside a specific organization.

Characteristics:
- collective
- frequently embedded in process
- sometimes formalized, often not
- often culturally specific

Promotion path: organizational recognition → governed evaluation → tenant-wide activation → cross-tenant candidate if universally applicable

### 3. Network Heuristics
Judgment patterns that have proven effective across multiple organizations, contexts, or use cases.

Characteristics:
- cross-tenant validated
- governed by explicit admission criteria
- available to all eligible tenants
- compounding as more organizations validate or refine them

Promotion path: multi-tenant validation → GAQP corroboration standard applied → network-level admission → version-controlled and revisable

---

## The Promotion Ladder

```
Individual Observation
  → Operator Heuristic Candidate
    → Tenant-Level Heuristic (if generalizable within org)
      → Network Heuristic Candidate (if applicable across tenants)
        → Network-Level Heuristic (if corroborated and admitted)
          → Canonical Network Intelligence (if durable and widely applicable)
```

---

## The Compounding Moat

This model creates a compounding qualitative intelligence network.

As more organizations use Execalc:
1. More local heuristics are observed
2. More pass promotion through the governed pathway
3. The network-level heuristic inventory grows
4. Each new organization benefits from existing promoted heuristics on day one
5. Each new organization also contributes to future promotion cycles

The result: **the value of the network grows with scale, and the intelligence is governed — not raw crowdsourced noise.**

This is structurally different from simple "training data aggregation" because:
- admission is governed, not automatic
- provenance is preserved
- heuristics carry confidence and corroboration metadata
- revision is tracked with lineage

---

## Governance Requirements

For heuristic promotion to remain trustworthy:

1. **Provenance must be preserved** — where did this heuristic originate?
2. **Scope must be declared** — what contexts does this apply to, and what does it not apply to?
3. **Corroboration must be tracked** — how many independent instances support this?
4. **Conflicts must be surfaced** — does this contradict a heuristic already in the network?
5. **Revisions must preserve lineage** — if the heuristic is refined, the prior version must be retained

## Relationship to the EKE Corpus

Network heuristics that pass the promotion ladder are candidates for admission to the Executive Knowledge Engine corpus.

Within the EKE, they become:
- callable at runtime
- activatable by scenario match
- composable with other frameworks and heuristics

The EKE corpus is the governed repository. Network heuristic promotion is the governed supply pathway that feeds it.

---

## Tenant Safety

Cross-tenant heuristic promotion must never expose proprietary organizational content.

The heuristic itself is promoted — not the underlying case, document, or conversation that produced it.

The system must:
- separate the heuristic from its source context
- evaluate the heuristic in abstracted form
- admit only the abstracted, governe form to the network layer
- preserve source attribution as metadata without exposing source content

## Thesis

The network heuristic promotion model is what turns Execalc from a single-tenant tool into a **compounding organizational intelligence infrastructure**.

Each organization that uses the system well makes the system slightly better for every organization that comes after.

That is not a network effect in the traditional sense — it is a **governed intelligence compounding model**, where scale increases not just reach but reasoning quality.
