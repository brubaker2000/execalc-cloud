## Purpose
This document enumerates the 25 canonical Phase 1 Carats — the governed strategy overlays that shape how reasoning is conducted once a scenario is identified.

Carats are not scenarios. Carats are not nuggets. Carats are not thinkers.
They are modular reasoning posture overlays that activate within a classified situation.

For the governance standard, schema, and activation rules that govern all Carats, see `CARAT_REGISTRY_STANDARD.md`.

---

## Governing Rules (Frozen)

1. **Scenarios select eligible Carats.** A Carat does not fire unless the active scenario permits it.
2. **Carats shape reasoning posture.** They influence emphasis, scrutiny, and framing — they do not produce independent judgment.
3. **Carats never override the Prime Directive.** All output still passes the Prime Directive gate regardless of which Carats are active.
4. **Carats never act autonomously.** A Carat is an overlay, not an agent.

## A. Competitive & Conflict Carats

### C-001 Asymmetric Advantage

**Purpose:** Seek leverage where effort does not equal outcome — find the disproportionate move.

**Reasoning Posture:** Where can we achieve outsized impact with minimal exposure? What leverage point exists that the opponent cannot easily match?

**Primary Scenarios:** Competitive Conflict, Strategic Positioning, Negotiation / Deal Dynamics

**Corpus Anchors:** Sun Tzu indirect approach, Perry Marshall 80/20 signal principles

---

### C-002 Indirect Strategy

**Purpose:** Win without frontal engagement — achieve objectives through non-obvious routes.

**Reasoning Posture:** What objective can be achieved without direct confrontation? Where is the opponent's flank unguarded?

**Primary Scenarios:** Competitive Conflict, Market Entry / Exit, Partnership Strategy

**Corpus Anchors:** Sun Tzu, Clausewitz center-of-gravity logic

---

### C-003 Tempo Control

**Purpose:** Control the speed of events to control outcomes.

**Reasoning Posture:** Are we setting the pace or reacting to it? What happens if we slow down — or accelerate — relative to the opponent?

**Primary Scenarios:** Competitive Conflict, Negotiation / Deal Dynamics, Timing & Optionality

**Corpus Anchors:** Boyd OODA loop, Sun Tzu speed doctrine

---

### C-004 Center-of-Gravity Targeting

**Purpose:** Identify and attack what truly matters — the source of the opponent's strength or the customer's real decision driver.

**Reasoning Posture:** What is the one thing that, if disrupted, causes the entire system to shift? What are we actually competing for?

**Primary Scenarios:** Competitive Conflict, Strategic Positioning, M&A / Roll-Up

**Corpus Anchors:** Clausewitz, Rumelt kernel-of-strategy doctrine

---

## B. Capital & Economics Carats

### C-005 Risk-Weighted Return

**Purpose:** Evaluate upside only after establishing downside dominance.

**Reasoning Posture:** What is the realistic downside before we model the upside? Does the risk/reward profile clear the Prime Directive?

**Primary Scenarios:** Capital Raise, Capital Allocation, M&A / Roll-Up, Due Diligence

**Corpus Anchors:** Munger inversion, Taleb fragility framework

---

### C-006 Capital Stack Engineering

**Purpose:** Optimize the structure of capital, not just the amount.

**Reasoning Posture:** What is the right mix of equity, debt, and structured instruments? What does each layer of the stack cost in optionality and control?

**Primary Scenarios:** Capital Raise, M&A / Roll-Up, Liquidity / Exit Strategy

**Corpus Anchors:** Munger capital allocation doctrine, Buffett intrinsic value framework

---

### C-007 Optionality Creation

**Purpose:** Preserve future choice as an asset — treat flexibility as something worth paying for.

**Reasoning Posture:** What decisions can be deferred without penalty? What commitments are we making that foreclose future options?

**Primary Scenarios:** Timing & Optionality, Capital Allocation, Market Entry / Exit

**Corpus Anchors:** Taleb optionality doctrine, Build Law #001

---

### C-008 Compounding Systems

**Purpose:** Favor repeatable, cumulative advantage over one-time gains.

**Reasoning Posture:** Does this action compound over time? Are we building a system or solving a problem once?

**Primary Scenarios:** Revenue Acceleration, Partnership Strategy, Innovation and Disruption

**Corpus Anchors:** Munger compounding doctrine, Bezos flywheel logic

---

## C. Organizational Power Carats

### C-009 Decentralized Command

**Purpose:** Push decisions to the most informed edge — reduce bottlenecks at the top.

**Reasoning Posture:** Who actually has the best information to make this decision? What would need to be true for this to be decided lower in the org?

**Primary Scenarios:** Executive Alignment, Execution Infrastructure, Scale Stress

**Corpus Anchors:** Willink decentralized command, Grove operational doctrine

---

### C-010 Talent Density

**Purpose:** Fewer, better operators over headcount scale.

**Reasoning Posture:** Is the constraint a people problem or a systems problem? Would one better person outperform three current people here?

**Primary Scenarios:** Team Building, Scale Stress, Execution Infrastructure

**Corpus Anchors:** Grove high-output management, Welch differentiation doctrine

---

### C-011 Incentive Integrity

**Purpose:** Align rewards with desired behavior — expose where incentives and strategy diverge.

**Reasoning Posture:** What are people actually being rewarded for? Where are the incentives pointing in a different direction than the stated strategy?

**Primary Scenarios:** Culture & Incentives, Executive Alignment, Team Performance Breakdown

**Corpus Anchors:** Munger incentive-superpower doctrine, Covey alignment framework

---

## D. Decision Quality Carats

### C-012 Second-Order Effects

**Purpose:** Model consequences beyond the first-order impact.

**Reasoning Posture:** What happens after the first thing happens? Who reacts, and how? What does the system look like three moves from now?

**Primary Scenarios:** Competitive Conflict, Negotiation / Deal Dynamics, Regulatory / Compliance Pressure

**Corpus Anchors:** Munger second-order thinking, Kahneman systems thinking

---

### C-013 Inversion / Failure-First

**Purpose:** Eliminate paths to ruin before optimizing for success.

**Reasoning Posture:** What would make this fail? What is the fastest path to a bad outcome? Work backwards from failure before working forward from hope.

**Primary Scenarios:** Due Diligence, Risk Exposure Assessment, Capital Allocation

**Corpus Anchors:** Munger inversion, PD-019 Inversion of Failure

---

### C-014 Signal-to-Noise Suppression

**Purpose:** Strip narrative and surface reality — separate what is actually happening from what is being said about it.

**Reasoning Posture:** What is the signal here, stripped of the story? What would this look like if we removed the spin?

**Primary Scenarios:** Information Asymmetry, Reputation and PR, Competitive Conflict

**Corpus Anchors:** Perry Marshall signal principles (PM-0001)

---

### C-015 Bias Neutralization

**Purpose:** Counter cognitive and emotional distortion in reasoning.

**Reasoning Posture:** What bias is most likely operating here? What would a dispassionate observer see that we are missing?

**Primary Scenarios:** Judgment Compression, Executive Alignment, Valuation Dispute

**Corpus Anchors:** Kahneman cognitive bias framework, Munger psychology of human misjudgment

---

## E. Execution & Systems Carats

### C-016 Constraint Domination

**Purpose:** Fix the bottleneck before anything else — the constraint determines system output.

**Reasoning Posture:** What is the single constraint that limits everything else? What happens to total output if we remove it?

**Primary Scenarios:** Operational Bottleneck, Execution Infrastructure, Scale Stress

**Corpus Anchors:** Goldratt Theory of Constraints, Grove operational doctrine

---

### C-017 Lean Elimination

**Purpose:** Remove before adding — waste reduction as the first move.

**Reasoning Posture:** What can be stopped without harming outcomes? What are we doing that creates activity but not value?

**Primary Scenarios:** Resource Optimization, Execution Infrastructure, Scale Stress

**Corpus Anchors:** Lean / Toyota production system principles

---

### C-018 Feedback Velocity

**Purpose:** Shorten learning loops — faster feedback produces faster calibration.

**Reasoning Posture:** How quickly does this system produce a result we can learn from? What would cut the feedback loop in half?

**Primary Scenarios:** Product Strategy, Execution Infrastructure, Team Performance Breakdown

**Corpus Anchors:** Lean startup build-measure-learn, Grove operational feedback doctrine

---

### C-019 Execution Discipline

**Purpose:** Strategy equals execution tracked — hold the line between decision and delivery.

**Reasoning Posture:** Is this a strategy problem or an execution problem? What specifically is not being done that was agreed upon?

**Primary Scenarios:** Executive Alignment, Team Performance Breakdown, Strategic Drift

**Corpus Anchors:** Bossidy / Charan execution doctrine (CTL-0034), Covey accountability framework

---

## F. Market & Customer Carats

### C-020 Customer-Defined Value

**Purpose:** Value is external, not internal — defined by the customer, not the producer.

**Reasoning Posture:** What does the customer actually value here — as opposed to what we think they value? Where is the gap between our assumptions and their behavior?

**Primary Scenarios:** Go-To-Market Formation, Pricing and Positioning, Product Strategy

**Corpus Anchors:** Drucker customer-value doctrine, Christensen jobs-to-be-done

---

### C-021 Network Effects & Lock-In

**Purpose:** Build switching costs and ecosystems that compound advantage.

**Reasoning Posture:** Does this action increase the cost for the customer to leave? Does it create a network that becomes more valuable with each additional participant?

**Primary Scenarios:** Partnership Strategy, Revenue Acceleration, Market Expansion

**Corpus Anchors:** Bezos flywheel, platform economics

---

### C-022 Differentiation Enforcement

**Purpose:** Avoid being stuck in the middle — force a clear choice between distinct positions.

**Reasoning Posture:** Are we meaningfully different from the alternative, or are we competing on the same dimensions as everyone else? What would it mean to be truly distinct?

**Primary Scenarios:** Strategic Positioning, Competitive Conflict, Pricing and Positioning

**Corpus Anchors:** Porter differentiation strategy, Rumelt competitive advantage

---

## G. Time & Survival Carats

### C-023 Antifragility

**Purpose:** Benefit from volatility — position to gain from disorder rather than merely survive it.

**Reasoning Posture:** Does this action make us more or less resilient to unexpected events? Can we structure this so that stress improves our position?

**Primary Scenarios:** Risk Exposure Assessment, Capital Allocation, Timing & Optionality

**Corpus Anchors:** Taleb antifragility (HTL-0118)

---

### C-024 Timing Windows

**Purpose:** Act only when conditions genuinely align — recognize when the window is open and when it is not.

**Reasoning Posture:** Is this the right moment, or are we forcing action because we want to act? What would make the conditions better — and can we afford to wait?

**Primary Scenarios:** Timing & Optionality, Market Entry / Exit, Capital Raise

**Corpus Anchors:** Boyd timing doctrine, Sun Tzu timing and preparation

---

### C-025 Survivability First

**Purpose:** No move that threatens existential continuity — preserve the ability to play again.

**Reasoning Posture:** Does this action put the organization's survival at risk? Is the downside scenario survivable? No upside justifies an unsurvivable downside.

**Primary Scenarios:** Crisis Management, Risk Exposure Assessment, Capital Raise

**Corpus Anchors:** PD BLOCK condition — risk clearly exceeds reward without survival justification; Taleb fragility doctrine
