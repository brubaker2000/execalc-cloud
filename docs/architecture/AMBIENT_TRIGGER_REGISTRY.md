## Status

## Owner
Executive Knowledge Engine (EKE)

## Position in Activation Cascade

Ambient triggers are the **first layer** of the EKE activation cascade. They fire before scenario detection, before Carats, before the Prime Directive evaluation.

```
Ambient Triggers (priming layer)
  → Scenario Detection (what situation?)
    → Carats (which overlays?)
      → EKE Corpus + Prime Directive
        → PSA Mode 1 (implication scan)
          → Judgment Call
            → Recursive Analysis Mode 1 (output review)
```

---

## Canonical Definition

Ambient triggers are pre-scenario signals that prime the system's attentional posture and reflex orientation before any situation has been formally classified.

Scenario detection answers: **What situation are we in?**

Ambient triggers answer a prior question: **What background condition is forming that should shape how we enter that situation?**

---

## Why This Layer Exists

Not every session begins with enough signal to classify a scenario. An operator may open a session with a diffuse sense that something is forming — an opportunity window, a drift, a risk building — before they can name the situation.

Without ambient triggers, the system enters every session blank, relying on explicit operator framing to determine orientation. With ambient triggers, the system scans background conditions first and arrives at scenario detection already primed.

This is the grandmaster analogy made operational: before speed chess begins, the grandmaster has already scanned the board. Ambient triggers are the board scan.

**What ambient triggers are:**
- Background condition detections that precede scenario classification
- Posture and reflex priming signals
- Early-warning and early-opportunity cues that change what the system looks for

**What ambient triggers are not:**
- Scenarios — they do not classify a situation; they orient the system before classification
- Carats — they are not applied within a scenario; they fire before one is named
- PSA Mode 1 implications — those are evidence-specific inferences drawn during reasoning from the full context package; ambient triggers are session-entry detections from background signals
- Memory — they are runtime detections, not stored objects

---

## The Two Classes

### Positive Ambient Triggers
Early-opportunity signals indicating upside is forming. Prime opportunity amplification, timing urgency, and asymmetric upside scanning.

### Preventive Ambient Triggers
Early-warning signals indicating downside is forming. Prime the "what am I not seeing?" discipline, liability awareness, and survivability evaluation.

The split is not binary in operation — a session may activate multiple triggers from both classes simultaneously. The system holds both simultaneously and adjusts output posture accordingly.

## The 20 Canonical Ambient Triggers

---

### Positive Triggers — Early-Opportunity, Upside-Forming

---

#### AT-P01: Momentum Signal

**What it detects:** The operator's language, recent decisions, or context reflect a streak of wins, expanding optionality, or a favorable run of conditions. The background state is positive.

**Detection signals:**
- Recent referenced outcomes have been favorable
- Operator language reflects confidence or acceleration
- Sequential decisions have compounded rather than cancelled

**Primes:**
- Asymmetric upside scanning — are there opportunities to amplify the current run?
- Timing urgency — momentum windows close; the system asks whether the operator is maximizing this window
- Doubling-down logic — which existing bets deserve more resource commitment now?

**PD Lens:** Risk/reward (upside open; floor established by prior wins)

---

#### AT-P02: Demand Signal Emergence

**What it detects:** Unsolicited inbound interest, pull from the market, or increasing external pressure without operator-initiated push. Supply/demand is moving in the operator's favor without the operator having forced it.

**Detection signals:**
- Inbound inquiries referenced without prior outbound effort
- Market or counterparty interest described as spontaneous
- Multiple independent parties signaling interest in similar timeframe

**Primes:**
- Pricing power assessment — demand signal without supply constraint means the operator may be underpriced
- Capacity evaluation — can the operator meet this demand at current resource levels?
- Supply/demand lens activation — structural imbalance is forming; how is it being leveraged?

**PD Lens:** Supply/demand

---

#### AT-P03: Relationship Capital Elevation

**What it detects:** A high-value relationship is warming, deepening, or moving toward a significant milestone — a deal, a commitment, an alliance, a referral.

**Detection signals:**
- A named relationship is referenced with increased frequency or weight
- The tone around a key counterparty has shifted from transactional to collaborative
- A relationship is approaching a decision point or threshold event

**Primes:**
- Trust-leveraging posture — the moment to deploy relationship capital selectively
- Timing sensitivity — relationship capital is perishable; windows to act on warm relationships close
- Risk of overplaying — the system asks whether the operator is about to extract too much from a relationship that should be preserved

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities (relationship capital is a balance sheet asset)

---

#### AT-P04: Market Timing Window

**What it detects:** External signals — competitive, macro, regulatory, seasonal — indicate a window is opening that will not persist. The structure of the opportunity is temporarily favorable.

**Detection signals:**
- Competitor distraction, contraction, or absence referenced
- Regulatory window or policy shift creating a temporary opening
- Market timing signals: rate environment, cyclical position, policy change

**Primes:**
- Option value evaluation — how long does this window last, and what is it worth?
- Urgency framing — the system asks whether the operator understands the window is finite
- First-mover logic — what does the operator capture by moving now vs. waiting?

**PD Lens:** Supply/demand (structural imbalance is time-limited)

---

#### AT-P05: Capability Unlock

**What it detects:** A new resource, person, technology, authority, or relationship has recently become available that materially changes the operator's strategic position.

**Detection signals:**
- A new hire, partnership, or tool referenced as recently acquired
- A previously unavailable authority or budget has been granted
- A prior constraint has been removed

**Primes:**
- Strategic optionality expansion — what was previously impossible is now possible
- Deployment planning — what is the highest-value first use of this capability?
- Asymmetry creation — does this capability enable the operator to create a structural advantage?

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities (new asset has entered the position)

---

#### AT-P06: Asymmetry Recognition

**What it detects:** The operator is positioned — or could be — with structurally limited downside and open upside. The risk floor is visible or engineerable.

**Detection signals:**
- Described exposure is capped by contract, structure, or resource limit
- The described opportunity has multiple upside paths and a defined worst case
- The operator has pre-engineered a recovery option

**Primes:**
- Asymmetry preservation doctrine — do not allow execution decisions to erode the structural advantage
- Risk-floor logic — ensure the floor remains visible as commitments increase
- PD enforcement — risk/reward lens must be explicitly evaluated before commitment

**PD Lens:** Risk/reward

---

#### AT-P07: Competitive Vulnerability Detected

**What it detects:** A competitor is showing weakness, distraction, contraction, or public misstep. A market window is forming.

**Detection signals:**
- Competitor referenced as distracted, reorganizing, or losing talent
- Public competitor misstep referenced (product failure, PR event, leadership departure)
- Competitor withdrawal from a market or segment referenced

**Primes:**
- Market positioning — what position can the operator take in the space the competitor is vacating?
- Preemptive talent or customer acquisition — the window to capture displaced assets is short
- Offensive option evaluation — what move would a well-advised operator make in the next 90 days?

**PD Lens:** Supply/demand (competitor withdrawal shifts structural balance)

---

#### AT-P08: Network Effect Inflection

**What it detects:** A platform, distribution channel, or relationship network the operator participates in is approaching a threshold where compounding begins to accelerate.

**Detection signals:**
- Referenced platform or network growth described as accelerating
- Operator's participation in a network is described as recently increasing in value
- Third-party validation or network density crossing a threshold

**Primes:**
- Timing urgency — the window to establish a compounding position closes as the network matures
- Defensive positioning — what does the operator need to lock in before the inflection point?
- Compounding logic — what investment now produces disproportionate returns after inflection?

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities (network position is a compounding asset or liability depending on timing)

---

#### AT-P09: Alliance Signal

**What it detects:** A potential partner, investor, or strategic stakeholder is signaling openness to alignment through behavior — not just words.

**Detection signals:**
- A counterparty is proactively sharing information, introducing contacts, or creating access
- A potential partner has referenced mutual benefit without the operator having initiated the framing
- A previously distant stakeholder has become more reachable or responsive without explanation

**Primes:**
- Partnership structuring logic — is the operator ready to move from signal to structure?
- Term design — what terms would govern an alliance that is fair under current leverage conditions?
- Alignment architecture — what does each party need from the alliance, and are those needs compatible?

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities (alliance is a potential asset; misaligned alliance is a liability)

---

#### AT-P10: Clarity Convergence

**What it detects:** The operator is circling a major decision and multiple threads are converging. The system detects that the operator is close to being ready to decide — whether they have said so or not.

**Detection signals:**
- Multiple prior sessions have touched related themes without resolution
- The operator's framing of a question is tightening — fewer variables, clearer stakes
- Referenced timelines, pressures, or stakeholder expectations are converging on a decision point

**Primes:**
- Synthesis mode — the system shifts from analysis to decision crystallization
- Decision readiness assessment — what is the last piece of information the operator needs before deciding?
- PSA Mode 2 preparation — the execution design layer should be ready to fire immediately after the decision

**PD Lens:** All three (decision convergence requires full PD evaluation)

---

### Preventive Triggers — Early-Warning, Downside-Forming

---

#### AT-V01: Execution Drift Signal

**What it detects:** Delivery velocity is slowing, milestones are slipping, or the gap between plan and reality is widening — without the operator having formally acknowledged it.

**Detection signals:**
- Referenced timelines have shifted without explanation
- Deliverables described as in-progress in prior sessions remain in-progress
- The operator's language around execution has become vaguer or more qualified

**Primes:**
- Constraint identification — what is the actual blocker?
- Accountability review — who owns the slipping element, and is that ownership clear?
- Priority reset — is this drift a signal that the original plan was wrong, not just slow?

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities (execution drift is an accumulating liability)

---

#### AT-V02: Assumption Staleness

**What it detects:** A decision or ongoing plan was made under conditions that may no longer hold. Time has passed, market has moved, or new information contradicts the original premise.

**Detection signals:**
- A referenced prior decision was made more than one quarter ago without review
- Market conditions or competitive posture described today conflict with assumptions referenced in memory
- The operator is proceeding on a plan without acknowledging how conditions have changed

**Primes:**
- State validity check — the Recursive Analysis Mode 2 function at session level
- Revalidation posture — the system asks whether the original decision would be made the same way today
- PD lens re-evaluation — do the three lenses still produce the same verdict?

**PD Lens:** Risk/reward (assumptions embedded in risk assessments may be stale)

---

#### AT-V03: Relationship Cooling

**What it detects:** A previously warm or productive relationship is showing reduced engagement, slower response, or changed tone without explanation from the counterparty.

**Detection signals:**
- A key relationship referenced as active in prior sessions is now described as quiet or slow
- Response cadence from a counterparty has measurably decreased
- Tone around a key relationship has shifted from collaborative to transactional or absent

**Primes:**
- Trust deficit awareness — the cooling may reflect information the counterparty has that the operator does not
- Information asymmetry check — what does the other party know or believe that is driving the change?
- Relationship triage — is this relationship worth active recovery, or should it be released?

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities (relationship capital is depreciating)

---

#### AT-V04: Concentration Risk Forming

**What it detects:** Too much exposure — revenue, relationship, resource, or attention — is converging on a single point. Structural fragility is building.

**Detection signals:**
- A single customer, partner, or channel accounts for a disproportionate share of referenced activity
- The operator's plan depends on a single person, resource, or event going well
- Prior diversification has eroded without replacement

**Primes:**
- Fragility test — what happens if the concentrated element fails, exits, or changes terms?
- Liability engineering principle — the operator should pre-engineer the downside before concentration compounds
- PD lens — assets/liabilities: concentration is a hidden liability that doesn't appear until it triggers

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities

---

#### AT-V05: Stakeholder Misalignment Signal

**What it detects:** Different parties in the operator's orbit are optimizing for different objectives without a reconciliation mechanism in place.

**Detection signals:**
- Multiple stakeholders referenced with different success definitions for the same initiative
- A decision described as agreed is later referenced with implicit resistance from a party who agreed
- Incentive structures for different parties point in different directions

**Primes:**
- Incentive mapping — what is each stakeholder actually optimizing for?
- Conflict identification — where do those objectives collide, and when does the collision become visible?
- Alignment architecture — what structure would reconcile the misaligned incentives before they break the initiative?

**PD Lens:** Risk/reward (misalignment is an unpriced execution risk)

---

#### AT-V06: Burn Rate / Runway Compression

**What it detects:** Financial, temporal, or human resources are contracting relative to commitments. The survivability window is narrowing without the operator having formally named it.

**Detection signals:**
- Referenced timelines or budgets are tightening
- The number of viable options is contracting as resources decline
- The operator's language reflects urgency without explicit acknowledgment of the constraint

**Primes:**
- Survivability test (PD-022) — is the worst-case outcome still survivable at current burn rate?
- Liability capping — what commitments should not be made until runway is secured?
- Priority triage — given the constraint, what is the single highest-leverage action?

**PD Lens:** Risk/reward and assets/liabilities

---

#### AT-V07: Narrative Drift

**What it detects:** The story the operator tells about their position, strategy, or situation has diverged from the evidence in memory or current observable reality. The framing has rotted without anyone noticing.

**Detection signals:**
- Operator's stated position conflicts with memory artifacts from prior sessions
- The rationale given for a current action contradicts the rationale given when the action was initiated
- The operator is describing a situation in terms that the evidence does not support

**Primes:**
- Authenticity check — GAQP Principle 3: claims must reflect what was actually observed, not what was hoped
- Assumption audit — what belief is driving the drift, and when was it last tested?
- Strategic coherence review — does the current narrative produce a coherent decision under the Prime Directive?

**PD Lens:** All three (narrative drift corrupts all PD lens evaluations simultaneously)

---

#### AT-V08: Key Person Dependency

**What it detects:** Critical capability, relationship, or authority is concentrated in one person, creating structural fragility if that person departs, is unavailable, or changes alignment.

**Detection signals:**
- A single named individual is referenced as the owner of multiple critical functions
- A relationship or access point described as essential is tied to one person's continued involvement
- The operator's plan has no named backup or succession for a critical role

**Primes:**
- Succession logic — what happens to the capability or relationship if this person is removed?
- Dependency mapping — how many critical paths run through this single node?
- Liability lens — key person dependency is a contingent liability that rarely appears on the balance sheet

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities

---

#### AT-V09: Decision Debt Accumulation

**What it detects:** Multiple significant decisions have been deferred without resolution. The backlog is generating compounding constraint, organizational friction, and narrowing future options.

**Detection signals:**
- The same unresolved question has appeared across multiple prior sessions
- Decisions described as pending in memory have not been resolved and are not being actively engaged
- The operator is making new decisions while prior decisions remain open and unresolved

**Primes:**
- Decision triage — which deferred decision is blocking the most other decisions?
- Governance gap identification — is this a PSA governance gap (surfaced three times without resolution)?
- PSA Mode 1 escalation — unresolved decisions that meet the governance gap threshold surface at elevated priority

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities (decision debt is an accumulating liability on the decision balance sheet)

---

#### AT-V10: Regulatory / Compliance Exposure

**What it detects:** The operator's activities may be approaching or inside a regulatory boundary that has not been formally evaluated. The risk is present but unnamed — not yet a crisis, not yet acknowledged.

**Detection signals:**
- Activities described touch regulatory domains without compliance review being referenced
- The operator is moving at speed in a space where regulatory constraints are known to exist
- Prior sessions show no compliance cartridge activation for activities that warrant it

**Primes:**
- Compliance cartridge activation — the appropriate compliance overlay should load before judgment proceeds
- Risk surface mapping — what are the specific exposure points, and which have not been evaluated?
- PD lens — assets/liabilities: unacknowledged regulatory exposure is a contingent liability that may be material

**PD Lens:** Assets/liabilities

  "notes": null

## Activation Rules

**Multiple triggers may fire simultaneously.** A session with both AT-P04 (Market Timing Window) and AT-V06 (Burn Rate Compression) active is a valid and important combination — it means the operator faces both an opportunity window and a resource constraint. The system holds both.

**Triggers do not suppress scenarios.** A fired ambient trigger primes orientation; it does not route to a specific scenario. Scenario detection runs after trigger priming.

**Triggers do not override Carats.** Carats are applied within a classified scenario. Triggers prime before classification.

**Positive and preventive triggers are additive, not competing.** The system does not choose between them. It holds the full picture of what is forming — upside and downside simultaneously.

**Trigger activation persists across a session.** Once fired at session entry, an ambient trigger remains active for the duration of the session unless explicitly suppressed by the operator.

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

| Framework | Relationship |
|---|---|
| **Scenario Registry** | Ambient triggers fire before scenario detection; they prime the orientation that enters scenario classification |
| **Carat Registry** | Carats are loaded after scenario detection; ambient triggers are pre-scenario |
| **PSA Mode 1** | Ambient triggers prime the implication scan; PSA Mode 1 is evidence-specific inference during reasoning; triggers set the attentional posture before reasoning begins |
| **Recursive Analysis Mode 2** | Longitudinal drift detection validates whether trigger assessments were accurate over time; AT-V02 (Assumption Staleness) is the session-level analog of RA Mode 2 state validity |
| **Heuristic Coding System** | Heuristics tagged with matching Dynamic Class tags may auto-activate when an ambient trigger fires (e.g., AT-V03 fires → trust_deficit dynamic class heuristics elevated) |
| **Prime Directive** | Every ambient trigger carries a primary PD lens; the trigger primes which lens deserves early attention in the Prime Directive evaluation |

---

## Design Principle

> Scenarios tell the system what room it is in. Carats tell it which tools to use. Ambient triggers tell it what to look for before it opens the door.

The operator should never have to explicitly name their attentional posture. The system reads the background and arrives at the conversation already oriented.
