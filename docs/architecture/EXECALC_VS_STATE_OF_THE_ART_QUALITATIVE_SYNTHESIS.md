# Execalc vs. State of the Art in Qualitative Synthesis

## 1. Purpose

This document defines Execalc’s position relative to the current state of the art in qualitative synthesis.

It establishes:
- what the field currently does well
- where it falls short
- where Execalc is designed to lead

---

## 2. What the Field Has Solved

Modern qualitative synthesis—both classical and AI-assisted—has made significant progress in the following areas:

### 2.1 Fragment Processing
Systems can:
- ingest large corpora of text
- segment documents into units of meaning
- perform initial coding and labeling

### 2.2 Pattern Detection (First Pass)
LLMs and related systems can:
- identify recurring phrases and ideas
- cluster semantically similar passages
- generate high-level themes

### 2.3 Retrieval and Context Expansion
Architectures such as RAG and GraphRAG enable:
- retrieval of relevant fragments
- entity and relationship mapping
- community detection across documents

These capabilities allow machines to operate at a scale that is infeasible for human analysts.

---

## 3. Where the Field Breaks Down

Despite progress, current systems exhibit critical limitations.

### 3.1 Shallow Theme Generation
LLMs tend to:
- produce generic themes
- collapse distinct concepts into single categories
- miss deeper structural relationships

### 3.2 Lack of Reproducibility
Outputs are often:
- inconsistent across runs
- sensitive to prompt phrasing
- difficult to validate

### 3.3 Weak Contradiction Detection
Systems struggle to:
- identify conflicting signals
- separate noise from signal
- recognize when outliers are more important than averages

### 3.4 Absence of Reflexive Judgment
Current systems lack:
- awareness of their own assumptions
- ability to question their outputs
- theoretical grounding

### 3.5 No Governance Layer
Most systems do not answer:
- Is this synthesis valid?
- Is it economically meaningful?
- Is it decision-relevant?

They produce plausible outputs, not governed conclusions.

---

## 4. The Current Paradigm

The dominant model in 2025–2026 is:

> AI-assisted qualitative analysis

Where:
- the LLM performs coding, clustering, and initial synthesis
- the human performs interpretation and validation

This is best understood as:
> AI as research assistant
