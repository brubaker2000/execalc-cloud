# GAQP Founding Charter
## Generally Accepted Qualitative Principles

---

## Founding Statement

GAQP is to qualitative judgment what GAAP is to financial accounting.

GAAP disciplined money. GAQP disciplines meaning.

For a century, organizations have had a shared standards framework for quantitative reporting. Numbers became comparable, auditable, and trustworthy across organizations, industries, and time periods because GAAP provided the common discipline.

Qualitative judgment — the reasoning behind strategy, hiring, market positioning, risk assessment, and organizational decision-making — has never had an equivalent.

GAQP is that standard.

---

## What GAQP Is

GAQP is a **standards framework for qualitative reasoning**.

It defines how claims derived from qualitative analysis should be:
- extracted
- classified
- tested for admission
- attributed to their source
- stored with provenance
- revised with lineage preserved
- applied to decisions with traceability

GAQP does not govern raw language. It governs **claims extracted from language**.

---

## What GAQP Is Not

GAQP is not:
- a writing style guide
- a prompt engineering framework
- a document summarization system
- an AI evaluation rubric
- a consulting methodology

GAQP is the explicit, portable, platform-native standard for qualitative reasoning discipline that consulting firms do privately and manually but have never made explicit or portable.

McKinsey and BCG do proto-GAQP. They apply rigorous qualitative discipline internally. But that discipline lives in culture, training, and proprietary process — not in a portable, explicit standard.

GAQP is the version that can be institutionalized, automated, and applied at scale.

---

## The Standards Body Model

Execalc is not merely a product that uses GAQP. Execalc is building GAQP.

The relationship is:
- **GAQP** is the doctrine
- **Execalc** is the operating system that enforces it

Just as GAAP required a standards body (FASB) to produce and maintain the principles, GAQP requires a governing body and a set of principles that can be versioned, revised, and extended deliberately.

GAQP v0.1 is the founding version. It is subject to deliberate revision. Principles should not be added casually.

## GAQP v0.1 — The Ten Core Principles

### 1. Materiality
Only signal that changes what a decision-maker would rationally do is worth capturing.

Not all language contains signal. GAQP-governed systems should filter for claims that would materially affect a decision, not merely for claims that are interesting or novel.

### 2. Traceability
Every claim must trace back to its source, context, and admission path.

A claim without provenance is not GAQP-compliant. The system must be able to answer: where did this come from, when was it observed, and how did it enter the system?

### 3. Authenticity
Claims must reflect what was actually observed, not what was hoped.

The system must preserve the difference between "this is what was said" and "this is what we wanted to hear." Authenticity is the antidote to motivated reasoning.

### 4. Context
No claim is fully interpretable without its originating context.

A statement extracted from its context can reverse in meaning or significance. GAQP-compliant storage must preserve enough context to make the claim interpretable without requiring access to the original document.

### 5. Separation of Observation and Interpretation
Raw observation and derived interpretation must be stored and labeled separately.

"The CFO expressed concern about margins" is an observation.  
"The company is facing a profitability crisis" is an interpretation.  
GAQP requires these to be distinct, not conflated.

### 6. Corroboration
Claims gain weight through independent confirmation; single-source claims carry lower confidence.

A claim supported by multiple independent sources is more reliable than a claim from one source. GAQP-compliant systems must track corroboration and assign confidence accordingly.

### 7. Explicit Assumptions
Assumptions embedded in claims must be named, not hidden.

Every analytical claim rests on assumptions. GAQP requires those assumptions to be surfaced and tagged as such — not buried as if they were established facts.

### 8. Revision
Claims must support structured revision with lineage preserved; revision is not deletion.

When a claim is updated, the prior version should be retained with a notation of what changed and why. GAQP treats revision as an upgrade, not an erasure.

### 9. Governed Memory Admission
Not all claims are admitted to active memory; admission requires a governed evaluation.

Capture and admission are separate acts. A claim may be observed and recorded without being admitted to active memory. Admission requires passing an explicit evaluation against admission criteria.

### 10. Reconstructable Decision Lineage
Any decision output must be reconstructable from its admitted claims, applied frameworks, and governing logic.

A GAQP-compliant decision is one that can be audited. Given the output, an observer should be able to trace back through the reasoning to understand what was known, what frameworks were applied, and what tradeoffs were accepted.

---

## Version Control

GAQP v0.1 defines these ten principles as the founding set.

Principles should be added, removed, or modified through a deliberate version revision process — not casually in the course of a chat or build session.

Versioning format: `GAQP vX.Y` where X is major revision and Y is minor.

## Thesis

GAQP will do for qualitative reasoning what GAAP did for financial reporting:

Create a shared, portable, explicit discipline that makes qualitative claims comparable, auditable, and trustworthy across organizations, time periods, and decision contexts.

The absence of GAQP has been invisible to most organizations because they have never had an alternative. Once the standard exists, the absence becomes legible — and the competitive advantage of operating under it becomes clear.
